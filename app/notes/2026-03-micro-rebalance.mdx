# Micro‑rebalance after three months

By the end of the third month, the portfolio had enough history to warrant a small rebalance. The intention from the start was to review allocations annually, but the data accumulated faster than expected. Some bets were clearly underused; others absorbed more time than planned without producing meaningful learning.

The review was kept deliberately simple. Calendar blocks for the past twelve weeks were tagged by bucket and compared to the intended mix. Core work held close to plan. Growth absorbed slightly more hours than expected, mostly from exploratory AI tooling. Distribution fell short, even after earlier adjustments. Meta time was present but fragmented, often split into five‑minute fragments instead of the planned, deeper sessions.

The micro‑rebalance made three changes. First, a small slice of Growth time was reassigned to Meta, creating one contiguous review block per week. Second, Distribution tasks were constrained further: no new channels, only updates that link existing work, videos, and the framework. Third, a trivial rule was added—no new Growth experiments unless the last one was written up in a short note like this.

None of these moves are dramatic on their own. The point is not to optimize every hour, but to make drift visible and respond with measured adjustments. This note exists as a checkpoint: a calm record that the portfolio can move in small steps without resetting the experiment or over‑fitting to short‑term noise.***
