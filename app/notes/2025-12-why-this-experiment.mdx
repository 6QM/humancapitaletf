This is a preface, not a field note. It explains why the experiment began before logging what happened inside it.

## The question I kept returning to

For a few years, I had been vaguely dissatisfied with how I thought about my own growth. Not because I wasn't learning—I was. But the learning felt disorganized in a way that bothered me. I'd spend months building one thing, then pivot to something adjacent, then circle back. Looking at the work from the outside, I couldn't always explain why the pieces connected.

At some point, I started thinking about it differently. I had a reasonably clear investment philosophy for financial capital: diversify, stay the course, rebalance on a schedule, don't react to short-term noise. That approach was calm and legible. Why didn't I have anything equivalent for my own skills and time?

## The obvious problem with self-improvement content

Most content about personal growth either pushes maximalist systems ("optimize every hour") or vague inspiration ("invest in yourself"). Neither felt like a useful operating model for someone who already works hard and just wants a clearer way to reason about the choices.

The ETF metaphor came from reading about index fund design. The core idea—passive discipline applied to a diversified basket of assets over a long time horizon—maps cleanly to how I'd like to manage learning: deliberate allocation, long holding periods on core skills, small explicit bets on growth areas, and periodic rebalancing rather than constant reaction.

## What the experiment is

The Human Capital ETF is not a productivity hack. It is a mental model and a record. The mental model is the framework. The record is these notes.

The goal is simple: over a multi-year period, does applying an investor's discipline to human capital produce a more legible, compounding portfolio of skills—or not? I don't know the answer. The experiment is how I find out.

## What it is not

- It is not a claim that this is the right approach for anyone else.
- It is not self-help content with a success story at the end.
- It is not a brand. The domain name is just a convenient label.

This note exists as a reference point. If the experiment runs for years and the results are mixed, I want a record of why I started and what I expected.
