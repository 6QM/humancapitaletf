# Setting the initial allocation

This note records the moment the Human Capital ETF experiment switched on. It is not a pitch and it is not a manifesto. It is simply a snapshot of how time, energy, and attention were allocated on day one.

The starting point was an engineering-heavy portfolio. Most working hours already sat inside **Core Assets**: building and operating software systems with a bias toward reliability and clear interfaces. The question was not whether to keep doing that work, but how explicit to be about its weight in the overall allocation.

For the first pass, the portfolio view looked like this:

- Core Assets: deep work on engineering and domain problems that already create value.
- Growth Assets: a defined slice of time for applied AI, data tooling, and new platforms.
- Distribution Layer: a light but consistent cadence of written notes and occasional video.
- Meta Layer: a small, protected block each week for review, learning design, and systems thinking.

Nothing about these buckets is exotic. What changed was the decision to treat them as a long-term structure rather than a weekly to‑do list. Each calendar block and project was tagged to a bucket, making it easier to see when activity drifted away from the intended mix.

This initial allocation is deliberately conservative. Core work remains the anchor; growth and distribution are present but not dominant. The expectation is that the mix will move over time as evidence accumulates, but that movement will be recorded and justified instead of happening quietly in the background. This note exists so there is a clear “before” to compare against later.***
