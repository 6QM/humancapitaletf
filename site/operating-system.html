PREFACE.

BEGIN WITH EVIDENCE

 

A framework becomes an operating system only when it changes a real allocation. Begin with evidence, not an ideal identity: the last four weeks of calendars, recovery, spending, work, learning, output, and unfinished commitments.

One page is enough for the first cycle. A dashboard can come later. Do not begin by inventing a perfect percentage for each position. Begin by seeing where resources already went, what they produced, and what risk is concentrated in one place.

The first rebalance begins with an honest inventory, not a new ambition.

SECTION 1.

INVENTORY

 

Inventory what exists before deciding what should exist. Record five layers.

Embodied Human Capital
Health, recovery, professional capability, transferable skills, learning capacity, and judgment.
Enabling and Externalized Assets
Financial buffer, tools, systems, reusable artifacts, reputation, relationships, and controlled channels.
Flows
Income, spending, feedback, opportunities, and recurring demands on time and energy.
Liabilities and Constraints
Health debt, financial obligations, unfinished commitments, family responsibilities, access limits, and contractual boundaries.
Concentration Exposures
Dependence on one employer, skill, internal system, platform, credential, country, income source, or identity.

A constraint is not a personal failure. It is a condition the policy must respect. A portfolio that ignores health, debt, caregiving, law, or employment obligations is not ambitious; it is mis-specified.

After the inventory, assign each item to its current primary function: Core, Growth, Distribution, or Meta. Do not force a permanent classification. The same capability may serve a different function after its maturity, use, or context changes.

Inventory question: What do I own or control, what only passes through me, and what can be taken away by one decision outside my control?

SECTION 2.

THE ASSET TEST

 

An activity can be useful without yet being an asset. Before placing the result on the asset side of the ledger, ask:

  1. Does anything remain after the labor is complete?
  2. Is it owned or meaningfully controlled by the worker?
  3. Can it be reused without being recreated from zero?
  4. Can it retain value in another employer, industry, platform, or environment?
  5. Has it produced stronger capability, a reusable artifact, income resilience, feedback, or opportunity?

A course may be an input. A capability demonstrated in repeated work may be embodied human capital. A documented tool may be an externalized asset. Reach on a rented platform is not the same as ownership or control. A paycheck is a return flow; the financial buffer retained from it is an enabling asset.

Employment and ownership boundary. Employer-confidential information and employer-owned intellectual property do not become personal assets merely because a worker helped create them. Externalize the transferable method, not protected data, code, documents, designs, or customer information.

Failing one test does not make an activity worthless. It shows what is missing. Some work maintains Core. Some learning is exploration. Some care and rest need no asset justification at all. The test prevents an input, activity, result, and return from being counted as the same thing.

SECTION 3.

ALLOCATION POLICY

 

Human Capital ETF provides no universal ratio. A useful policy is one page long and specific to the worker's present stage, risks, obligations, and evidence.

Current Stage
The life and career condition for which this policy is written.
Principal at Risk
The health, income, employability, credibility, or financial-buffer weakness that could impair the whole system.
Core Obligation
What must be protected or restored before expansion is increased.
Growth Thesis
One capability or project expected to widen future choice, and the evidence that would validate it.
Distribution Commitment
One safe form in which useful capability will be externalized and tested.
Meta Review Rule
The evidence, date, and decision owner for the next review.
Position Priority
The position that receives marginal time, energy, attention, or money first in this phase.
Exploration Budget
The maximum loss of time, energy, or money permitted for uncertain experiments.
Constraints
Health, family, financial, legal, employment, and access conditions the policy must respect.
Not Pursued
Projects, platforms, courses, or identities that will receive no allocation during this policy period.
Stop or Exit Triggers
The evidence that will reduce, suspend, or end an allocation.

An allocation policy is meaningful only when it excludes something.

The policy should enter the calendar and budget. A declared priority with no protected time, no resource limit, and no stop rule is a preference, not an allocation.

SECTION 4.

REBALANCING

 

Rebalancing is not constant change. It separates observation from intervention so that a temporary fluctuation does not become a new life strategy.

Monthly Observation
Record drift, recovery, spending, project evidence, output, and concentrated exposure. Observe without trading every fluctuation.
Quarterly Rebalance
Add, increase, maintain, reduce, migrate, or exit projects and commitments according to accumulated evidence.
Annual Policy Review
Reconsider life stage, health, career direction, family responsibility, constraints, portfolio purpose, and the rules themselves.

Evidence can trigger an earlier review: sustained deterioration in recovery; a financial buffer crossing its minimum threshold; a Growth capability succeeding in repeated delivery; verified Distribution feedback; a change in employment conditions; or firm-specific exposure becoming too large to survive.

Add
A missing exposure or mechanism now deserves an initial allocation.
Increase
Evidence is stronger and justifies more resources.
Maintain
The position is functioning and needs no reactive change.
Reduce
Cost, risk, or declining usefulness no longer justifies the present allocation.
Exit
The thesis has failed, the constraint has changed, or the project no longer belongs in the policy.

Rebalancing question: What changed in the evidence—not merely in my mood?

SECTION 5.

ASSET MIGRATION

 

Migration is a change in function, not a promotion in status.

Growth to Core
A capability becomes reliable in repeated delivery and now protects employability, quality, or income continuity.
Core to Distribution
Tacit experience is safely externalized into an artifact that others can find, verify, and use.
Distribution to Supporting Assets
Useful artifacts build reputation, relationships, and controlled channels.
Distribution to Returns
Those assets generate feedback, opportunity, autonomy, or income.
Returns to Core or Growth
Money, feedback, and opportunity are reinvested in recovery, a financial buffer, professional capacity, or the next capability.
Meta to the Next Allocation
The governance layer reads the migration evidence and changes the policy.

Migration prevents double counting. An essay is not simultaneously the skill that produced it, the reputation it may build, the feedback it receives, and the income that might follow. Each belongs to a different layer, even when one causal chain connects them.

SECTION 6.

THE FIRST PORTFOLIO CYCLE

 

Start tomorrow with six decisions.

  1. Review the last four weeks of actual allocation.
  2. Name one immediate threat to Core.
  3. Select one portable Growth capability to test in a real project.
  4. Externalize one non-confidential method or artifact through Distribution.
  5. Write one rebalance trigger and one review date.
  6. Stop one activity that consumes inputs without supporting the current policy.

The first ninety days can then run as one complete cycle.

Month One
Complete the inventory, identify the principal at risk, and write the one-page allocation policy.
Month Two
Run one full loop: learn, deploy, externalize, receive feedback, and review.
Month Three
Perform the first rebalance: add or increase one item, maintain one, reduce or exit one, and migrate one only if the evidence supports it.

Do not attempt to design an entire life in ninety days. The purpose is to make Human Capital ETF operate once, leave a record, and improve the next allocation.

The goal is not to optimize every hour. It is to make the next allocation more deliberate and the future less fragile.
